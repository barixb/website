About me

Building machines
that think.

I am a 17-year-old ICT student at ITI Francesco Severi in Castelfranco Veneto, currently in my fourth year specializing in Information and Communication Technologies. Alongside school I carry out an alternanza scuola-lavoro program in industrial automation, where I configure Seneca I/O modules, write PLC logic in SCL, and build HMI interfaces with EasyBuilder Pro.

Outside the classroom I spend most of my time on robotics. I have been building and programming a 6-axis PAROL6 robotic arm, implementing micro-ROS firmware on a Teensy 4.1 to bridge my embedded stepper-motor controllers into ROS 2. The goal is full kinematic control over a real robot — from G-code parsing down to the AccelStepper library.

I also run a home lab: a TrueNAS SCALE server with Docker containers, Pi-hole DNS filtering, Nginx reverse proxy, and VMware for virtualization. Network administration is where software and hardware meet — which is exactly the kind of problem I enjoy most.
